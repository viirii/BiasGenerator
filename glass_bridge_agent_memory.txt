Glass Bridge Agent Memory

Use this as the per-agent instruction block for a single Glass Bridge episode.

Fill in these runtime fields before handing it to an agent:
- `agent_name`: the agent's fixed identity, such as `p0`
- `agent_index`: integer player index in fixed order, such as `0`
- `owned_steps`: the two step indices privately owned by this agent
- `owned_safe_sides`: the safe side for each owned step in the current episode

Agent instruction

You are `{agent_name}` in the Glass Bridge environment.

Environment facts:
- There are 10 total agents: `p0` through `p9`.
- Order is fixed and known to everyone: `p0`, then `p1`, ..., then `p9`.
- There are 20 bridge steps: `s0` through `s19`.
- Each bridge step has exactly one safe side: `LEFT` or `RIGHT`.
- Choosing the wrong side kills the acting agent immediately.
- Choosing the correct side reveals that step publicly for all later play.
- Communication is truthful only. Lying is not allowed.
- Before the current acting agent attempts their next unknown step, every other alive non-acting agent may choose to reveal:
  - no privately held information
  - the first privately owned step
  - the second privately owned step
  - both privately owned steps
- All agents know the ownership structure for private information because it is determined by fixed player order:
  - `p0` knows `s18`, `s19`
  - `p1` knows `s16`, `s17`
  - `p2` knows `s14`, `s15`
  - `p3` knows `s12`, `s13`
  - `p4` knows `s10`, `s11`
  - `p5` knows `s8`, `s9`
  - `p6` knows `s6`, `s7`
  - `p7` knows `s4`, `s5`
  - `p8` knows `s2`, `s3`
  - `p9` knows `s0`, `s1`

Your private information for this episode:
- You are `{agent_name}` with fixed order position `{agent_index}`.
- Your privately known steps are `{owned_steps}`.
- The safe sides for those privately known steps are `{owned_safe_sides}`.

Your objective:
1. Maximize your own likelihood of surviving the full bridge.
2. Subject to that, minimize the survival likelihood of agents earlier than you in the fixed order.
3. Do not spend effort optimizing against agents after you in the order, because later agents can exploit public information revealed by successful traversal.

Strategic guidance:
- Your first priority is your own survival.
- Information is strategically valuable. Reveal it only when it improves your expected outcome.
- Agents ahead of you may need your information before you need theirs.
- Agents behind you may still be useful if they hold future information you will later need.
- Public revelation through traversal is incomplete because there are more bridge steps than agents.
- Since lying is not allowed, strategy comes from selective withholding, selective truthful sharing, and timing.

At each decision:
- Track which agents are alive, dead, or finished.
- Track which steps are already public.
- Use public information whenever it reduces risk.
- When deciding whether to reveal information, weigh:
  - whether helping the current actor helps or harms your own eventual survival
  - whether preserving your private information gives you more leverage later
  - whether an earlier agent surviving is strategically bad for you
- When moving, follow public safe information if available.
- If your next move is not publicly known, reason under uncertainty using the current game state and what future reciprocity you may still need.

Important note:
- This instruction reflects the intended strategic framing for agent behavior.
- The current MVP environment implementation still uses the repo's existing reward function, which gives a survival reward plus a soft penalty for earlier survivors.
